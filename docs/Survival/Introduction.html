Introduction

Author
Affiliations

Nathan Constantine-Cooke

Centre for Genomic and Experimental Medicine

MRC Human Genetics Unit

Published

March 26, 2024

This report explores associations between time to flare and the variables recorded at baseline. Time to flare is modelled using Cox models with the variable of interest and controlled variables (sex, and deprivation) included as fixed effects. A gamma frailty term is employed for hospital sites (which may capture inter-hospital differences).

The primary study endpoint is soft flare which is defined as a participant reporting their disease as being uncontrolled when they completed a monthly study questionnaire.

For soft flares, only the first two years of follow-up are considered as this was the time interval in which questionnaires were sent to participants.

The secondary study endpoint is hard flare, which required elevated CRP and/or CRP and for the subject to change medication. Data for this endpoint was collected via end-of-study phenotyping.

For hard flares, the full study follow-up period has been used, from study recruitment to the time the end of study phenotyping was completed.

Soft and hard flares have been identified by Dr Linda Williams, the PREdiCCt trial statistician. The same flare definitions have been used across all teams working on PREdiCCt.
